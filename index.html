The Misinformation Mallet

Hello and welcome to The Misinformation Mallet! This is a webpage that is dedicated to keeping you aware of the dangers of misinformation on the internet. Misinformation has been plaguing the internet since the invention of online forums where people can share any information that they may find, and this webpage is here to tackle and debate this problem.

In this website, you can access the main content through the navigation bar above, and read the thoughts and ideas of yours truly.

This website was created as the project of a Computer Science 3 course by Josh Joner P. Calbang

Questions (Content)

What can I expect from this website?

The main features of this webpage are the 5 clickable buttons on the navigation bar that lead you to the homepage and the 4 other content pages of this website. Here are the descriptions of each of the clickable buttons of the website:

  • The Logo - It leads you to this homepage, useless in here but very useful when you are on the content pages.
  • Fun - It leads you a webpage that contains a satirical essay about the misinformation spread on the internet.
  • Information - Currently it doesn't lead to anything, but it is supposed to lead you to a webpage that contains a serious article about misinformation, unlike the Fun button.
  • Warning - Currently it also doesn't lead to anything, but it is supposed to lead you to a webpage that lists dangers about misinformation spreading on the net.
  • Call to Action - Currently it also doesn't lead to anything, but it is supposed to lead you to a webpage that has ways for you to stop/prevent the spreading of misinformation on the internet.

Why do you think that this webpage needs to be created?

Misinformation has been around since probably when information was able to be communicated to other people (a long time). Spreading information for malicious intent or because someone did not know any better can harm everyone involved. This matter is even more highlighted now when the internet was created and even more when the World Wide Web allowed you to share your thoughts to the world.

Why is it named "The Misinformation Mallet"

Alliteration. The main goal of this website is to spread the prevention of misinformation on the internet, and a mallet is a type of hammer used to smash objects with heavy, blunt force. This website tries to encapsulate that feeling by being some sort of mallet that can smash the spread of misinformation on the internet

catJAM? Any reasons why the clip of a cat bobbing its head is prominent in the website?

This webpage is not exactly a dedicated and serious one because of the fact that this is a project and a test to CSS and HTML skills. The catJAM clip serves as a wholesome placeholder for what would be actual links

The limitations are: if you are on a device which supports cursor control and is of the medium size, the navigation bar at the top will expand down, causing an asymmetrical result. On tablets, laptops and phones, that "bug" is not present due to the three devices either being too large or are supporting touch-screen.
